## I work with data teams, design orgs, and universities to close the gap between what data means and what people think it means.

Whether you need a speaker, a teacher, a terminology audit, or an advisor who's been in the trenches of AI-powered data products. Here's how we can work together.

I bring real stories from designing at Microsoft — the kind of talk where people leave saying *"oh, that's why our AI feels brittle"* or *"we need to redesign how decisions work."*

University courses and professional workshops at the intersection of content design and data engineering.

### Consulting

Terminology audits, naming system design, and data product content strategy.

I'll evaluate your data product's content layer — naming conventions, metadata quality, error messages, documentation, discoverability — and deliver actionable recommendations. Think of it as a content design review for your data infrastructure.

**Engagements include:**
- Terminology audit + style guide
- Naming convention design for schemas, metrics, and APIs
- Data catalog content strategy
- AI metadata quality assessment (are your column descriptions good enough for AI agents?)
- Error message and empty state redesign for data products

**Best for:** Data teams, platform teams, companies building semantic layers

---

### Advising

Ongoing advisory for content design teams expanding into data products.

If your content design team is starting to work on data-heavy products — dashboards, data catalogs, semantic models, AI-assisted analytics — and you want someone who's already navigated that transition, I can help your team build the skills, frameworks, and confidence to own the content layer of data infrastructure.

**Best for:** Design orgs scaling into technical domains, content teams on data platform products
